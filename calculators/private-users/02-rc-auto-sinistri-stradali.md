# RC Auto & Sinistri Stradali

## Live tool

The live orientative calculator is available on the official CalcuLegal website:

* [RC Auto & Sinistri Stradali](https://www.calculegal.it/calcolo-risarcimento-incidente-stradale)
* [Car Accident Compensation Calculator](https://www.calculegal.it/en/car-accident-compensation-calculator)

The live calculator is designed for end users who want to obtain a preliminary, structured and orientative simulation before deciding whether to seek professional evaluation.

## Purpose

This document describes the public methodology behind the CalcuLegal calculator for **RC Auto & Sinistri Stradali**.

The calculator is designed as a preliminary legal empowerment tool. It helps users structure relevant facts and understand potential categories of damage, compensation or risk in road-accident and motor-liability scenarios.

It is not a tool for establishing liability, reconstructing an accident, determining insurance coverage or replacing legal, medico-legal, technical or insurance-related evaluation.

## Keywords

road accidents, vehicle damage, personal injury, contributory fault, insurance context, motor liability, traffic accident, biological damage, material damage, insurance claim, road injury, compensation simulation

## Public methodology

The calculator follows a structured pattern:

1. identify the factual road-accident scenario;
2. collect legally relevant non-identifying inputs;
3. organize the case around accident dynamics, damage type, injury consequences and insurance context;
4. distinguish between personal injury, material damage and procedural/insurance-related elements;
5. apply deterministic reasoning or official/public parameters where appropriate;
6. consider relevant factors such as contributory fault, temporary injury, permanent impairment, expenses and documentary evidence;
7. return an orientative result or risk profile;
8. remind the user that professional evaluation remains necessary.

## Input categories

The live calculator may guide the user through structured categories such as:

* type of accident;
* role of the user in the accident;
* vehicle or pedestrian/cyclist involvement;
* preliminary accident dynamics;
* possible contributory fault;
* temporary injury period;
* permanent biological damage values, if known;
* medical expenses;
* material damage;
* loss of income or other economic consequences;
* insurance status;
* available documents;
* procedural status or previous communications.

This repository does not publish the complete questionnaire flow or the production logic used by the live platform.

## Output categories

The calculator may provide preliminary and orientative outputs such as:

* structured summary of the accident scenario;
* possible damage categories;
* preliminary compensation or risk profile;
* indication of factors affecting the simulation;
* documentary checklist;
* awareness-oriented explanation of limits and next steps.

Outputs are not intended to determine liability, reconstruct the accident, quantify a final claim or replace professional evaluation.

## Output nature

Outputs are preliminary, orientative and non-binding.

They are not legal advice, medico-legal advice, technical accident reconstruction, insurance advice, a professional opinion or a guarantee of outcome.

## Legal empowerment role

The purpose of this calculator is to help users understand whether their road-accident situation may require further professional evaluation.

It supports awareness, organization of information and preliminary orientation.

It does not replace:

* a lawyer;
* a medico-legal expert;
* an accident reconstruction expert;
* an insurance professional;
* a court-appointed expert;
* a professional assessment of liability, causation, coverage or damages.
