# Danno Differenziale INAIL / Civile

## Live tool

The live orientative calculator is available on the official CalcuLegal website:

* [Danno Differenziale INAIL / Civile](https://www.calculegal.it/calcolo-danno-differenziale)
* [Workers’ Compensation Differential Damage Calculator](https://www.calculegal.it/en/workers-compensation-differential-damage)

The live calculator is designed for end users who want to obtain a preliminary, structured and orientative simulation before deciding whether to seek professional evaluation.

## Purpose

This document describes the public methodology behind the CalcuLegal calculator for **Danno Differenziale INAIL / Civile**.

The calculator is designed as a preliminary legal empowerment tool. It helps users structure relevant facts and understand potential categories of civil damage, INAIL compensation, differential damage and legal-economic exposure.

It is not a tool for establishing employer liability, proving causation, determining the correct legal claim or replacing legal, medico-legal, accounting or labour-law evaluation.

## Public methodology

The calculator follows a structured pattern:

1. identify the workplace-injury, occupational-disease or civil-liability scenario;
2. collect legally relevant non-identifying inputs;
3. organize the case around INAIL recognition, civil damage categories, economic impact and responsibility context;
4. distinguish between INAIL compensation and civil-law compensation;
5. consider the principle of deduction by homogeneous damage items where relevant;
6. apply deterministic reasoning or official/public parameters where appropriate;
7. separate biological, patrimonial, moral, family-related, procedural and documentary dimensions;
8. return an orientative result or risk profile;
9. remind the user that professional evaluation remains necessary.

## Input categories

The live calculator may guide the user through structured categories such as:

* type of event or harmful exposure;
* workplace injury, occupational disease or third-party responsibility context;
* INAIL impairment percentage;
* civil-law biological damage percentage, if known;
* temporary injury period;
* INAIL compensation already recognized or paid;
* annuity or capital-payment information;
* income and earning-capacity elements;
* family or caregiver-related consequences;
* medical, assistance or rehabilitation expenses;
* moral or non-economic damage indicators;
* employer-responsibility or third-party-liability context;
* contributory fault elements;
* available documents;
* procedural status or previous actions.

This repository does not publish the complete questionnaire flow or the production logic used by the live platform.

## Output categories

The calculator may provide preliminary and orientative outputs such as:

* structured summary of the INAIL/civil scenario;
* preliminary indication of possible differential-damage categories;
* separation of civil and INAIL-related components;
* awareness-oriented explanation of homogeneous-item deduction logic;
* indication of factors that may affect the simulation;
* documentary checklist;
* explanation of limits and next steps.

Outputs are not intended to determine employer liability, prove causation, quantify a final claim or replace professional evaluation.

## Output nature

Outputs are preliminary, orientative and non-binding.

They are not legal advice, medico-legal advice, accounting advice, labour-law advice, a professional opinion, a liability assessment or a guarantee of outcome.

## Legal empowerment role

The purpose of this calculator is to help users understand whether their workplace-injury, occupational-disease or INAIL/civil-liability situation may require further professional evaluation.

It supports awareness, organization of information and preliminary orientation.

It does not replace:

* a lawyer;
* a labour-law professional;
* a medico-legal expert;
* an accountant or damages expert;
* a court-appointed expert;
* a professional assessment of liability, causation, compensation or damages.

## Methodological note: homogeneous damage items

In differential-damage scenarios, a central public concept is the distinction between INAIL compensation and civil-law compensation.

The public methodology refers to the idea that deductions should be evaluated by comparable or homogeneous damage items, rather than by mechanically subtracting all INAIL amounts from all civil-law damage categories.

This repository explains the concept at a methodological level only. It does not publish the production calculation engine, the complete internal logic or the case-specific evaluation rules used by the live platform.
