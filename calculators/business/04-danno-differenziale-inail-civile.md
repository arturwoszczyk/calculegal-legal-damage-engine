## Purpose

This document describes the public methodology behind the CalcuLegal calculator for **Danno Differenziale INAIL / Civile — Business**.

The calculator is designed as a preliminary legal empowerment and risk-awareness tool. It helps organizations structure relevant facts and understand potential categories of workplace-injury exposure, INAIL/civil differential damage, prevention duties, employer-responsibility risk and legal-economic impact.

It is not a tool for determining employer liability, certifying compliance with workplace-safety duties, proving causation, evaluating insurance coverage or replacing legal, labour-law, medico-legal, accounting, health-and-safety or technical evaluation.

## Public methodology

The calculator follows a structured pattern:

1. identify the workplace-injury, occupational-disease or employer-risk scenario;
2. collect legally relevant non-identifying inputs;
3. organize the case around INAIL recognition, civil-damage categories, prevention duties, company context and responsibility indicators;
4. distinguish between INAIL compensation, civil-law compensation, employer-risk exposure and organizational/prevention dimensions;
5. consider the principle of deduction by homogeneous damage items where relevant;
6. apply deterministic reasoning or official/public parameters where appropriate;
7. separate biological, patrimonial, moral, procedural, documentary, organizational and compliance-related dimensions;
8. return an orientative result or risk profile;
9. remind the user that professional evaluation remains necessary.

## Methodological note: homogeneous damage items and business exposure

In INAIL/civil differential-damage scenarios, a central public concept is the distinction between INAIL compensation and civil-law compensation.

The public methodology refers to the idea that deductions should be evaluated by comparable or homogeneous damage items, rather than by mechanically subtracting all INAIL amounts from all civil-law damage categories.

In the business context, the same scenario may also involve prevention duties, employer-responsibility exposure, documentation, insurance context, organizational safeguards and procedural risk.

This repository explains these concepts at a methodological level only. It does not publish the production calculation engine, the complete internal logic or the case-specific evaluation rules used by the live platform.
