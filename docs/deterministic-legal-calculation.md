# Deterministic Legal Calculation

CalcuLegal calculators are designed as deterministic systems.

A deterministic legal calculator is not a generative AI model. It does not predict a court decision, invent a legal opinion or simulate a lawyer.

It applies a structured set of inputs to predefined logical and mathematical rules.

## Why deterministic logic matters

Deterministic logic is important in legal-tech because it supports:

- reproducibility;
- transparency;
- explainability;
- auditability;
- user trust;
- reduced hallucination risk.

## Difference from generative AI

Generative AI may be useful for drafting, summarization or language assistance, but it is not suitable as the source of a compensation estimate unless constrained by reliable rules and professional supervision.

CalcuLegal’s public methodology treats calculation as a rules-based process.

## Output nature

Outputs are:

- preliminary;
- orientative;
- non-binding;
- dependent on user-provided data;
- not a substitute for legal or medico-legal assessment.

## Deterministic does not mean certain

A deterministic calculator may be internally consistent, but legal reality remains fact-sensitive.

Evidence, medical reports, witness statements, contractual documents, insurance terms, judicial interpretation and procedural deadlines can change the outcome.
