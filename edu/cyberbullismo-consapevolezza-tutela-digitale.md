# Cyberbullismo — Consapevolezza e Tutela Digitale

## Live module

The live CalcuLegal Edu module is available on the official CalcuLegal website:

* [Cyberbullismo — Consapevolezza e Tutela Digitale](https://www.calculegal.it/edu/cyberbullismo/)

The live module is designed for end users, schools, families, parents, teachers and vulnerable users who need preliminary awareness, prevention-oriented guidance and structured orientation in cyberbullying or digital-harm scenarios.

## Purpose

This document describes the public methodology behind the CalcuLegal Edu cyberbullying module.

The module is designed to support:

* people facing a real cyberbullying or digital violence situation;
* parents and teachers;
* schools;
* educational workshops;
* prevention activities;
* vulnerable users who need safe, low-friction orientation.

It does not provide legal advice and does not replace emergency services, schools, public authorities, psychologists, educators, trusted adults or lawyers.

It is an educational and awareness-oriented module, not a compensation calculator and not a legal-risk scoring tool for litigation.

## Two-path structure

The module is based on a two-path structure.

### 1. Pronto Soccorso Digitale

A real-case orientation path for users facing an urgent, concrete or emotionally sensitive situation.

Public categories may include:

* type of situation;
* role of the user;
* age of involved persons;
* duration;
* platform or digital context;
* optional description;
* evidence availability;
* urgency indicators;
* action-oriented report;
* institutional resources.

The purpose of this path is to help users understand what kind of situation they may be facing, what information may be relevant, whether evidence should be preserved and whether trusted adults, schools, institutions or professionals should be contacted.

It does not determine legal qualification, assign blame, assess criminal liability or replace emergency response.

### 2. Palestra Educativa

An educational simulator for schools, families and prevention activities.

Public components may include:

* legal myth thermometer;
* choice simulator;
* empathy and perception exercises;
* discussion prompts;
* consequences dashboard;
* awareness questions;
* reflection exercises;
* institutional-resource orientation.

The purpose of this path is to support prevention, emotional literacy, digital responsibility and legal awareness through interactive educational scenarios.

It does not replace school policies, psychological support, educational professionals or legal evaluation.

## Public methodology

The module follows a structured awareness pattern:

1. identify whether the user is facing a real situation or an educational/prevention scenario;
2. collect minimal, non-identifying and context-relevant inputs;
3. organize the case around role, age, platform, duration, evidence, urgency and potential harm;
4. distinguish between emotional impact, legal-awareness dimensions, safety needs and institutional-resource orientation;
5. provide a preliminary action-oriented or educational output;
6. remind the user that professional, institutional or emergency support may be necessary.

## Safety-first design

The module includes vulnerable-user interface principles and safety-oriented patterns such as:

* quick exit;
* SOS resources;
* low-friction access;
* minimal data collection;
* no unnecessary registration;
* clear disclaimers;
* age-sensitive language;
* non-blaming tone;
* evidence-preservation reminders;
* institutional-resource visibility;
* encouragement to contact trusted adults, schools, institutions or professionals where appropriate.

The safety-first design is intended to reduce friction, avoid unnecessary exposure and support users who may be in emotionally sensitive or vulnerable situations.

## Output categories

The module may provide preliminary and orientative outputs such as:

* structured summary of the situation;
* urgency or attention indicators;
* evidence-preservation reminders;
* safe-response suggestions;
* institutional-resource orientation;
* educational reflection prompts;
* awareness-oriented explanation of possible consequences;
* clarification of the limits of automated orientation.

Outputs are not intended to determine legal responsibility, classify a criminal offence, replace emergency services, provide psychological support or substitute professional evaluation.

## Output nature

Outputs are preliminary, orientative, educational and non-binding.

They are not legal advice, psychological advice, emergency intervention, school disciplinary assessment, forensic advice, platform-removal advice, a professional opinion or a guarantee of outcome.

## Methodological note: empathy, myths and choices

Cyberbullying and digital-harm scenarios often involve an empathy gap, normalization of harmful conduct, misunderstanding of legal consequences and underestimation of evidence or persistence.

The public methodology may include educational tools such as myth-checking, choice simulation, empathy exercises and consequence dashboards.

This repository documents these concepts at a methodological level only.

It does not publish the complete quiz database, full educational scenario database, exact scoring logic, production UX copy or complete interface flow.
