# CalcuLegal Edu

CalcuLegal Edu extends CalcuLegal from damage estimation to prevention, awareness and early orientation.

It is designed for schools, families, vulnerable users and educational contexts.

CalcuLegal Edu is not a damage calculator. It is an interactive legal-awareness and prevention area focused on helping users recognize risk situations, understand possible consequences, preserve evidence where appropriate and identify institutional or professional resources.

## Live Edu area

The live CalcuLegal Edu area is available on the official CalcuLegal website:

* [CalcuLegal Edu](https://www.calculegal.it/edu/)
* [Cyberbullismo — Consapevolezza e Tutela Digitale](https://www.calculegal.it/edu/cyberbullismo/)

The live Edu modules are designed for end users, schools, families and vulnerable users who need preliminary awareness and structured orientation, not legal advice or binding professional assessment.

## Difference from calculators

CalcuLegal calculators estimate damages or legal-economic exposure.

CalcuLegal Edu modules focus on:

* prevention;
* digital safety;
* legal awareness;
* emotional literacy;
* early orientation;
* institutional resources;
* evidence-preservation awareness;
* safe-response orientation;
* educational simulation.

Edu modules are designed to support awareness before escalation.

They do not replace lawyers, educators, psychologists, public authorities, school staff, emergency services or professional support networks.

## Methodological positioning

CalcuLegal Edu is designed around structured awareness workflows, not generative legal advice.

The modules may use interactive questions, educational scenarios, myth-checking, choice simulation and empathy-oriented reflection to help users understand the seriousness of a situation.

The goal is not to classify a final legal case or determine liability.

The goal is to help users recognize risk, organize relevant information, preserve evidence where appropriate and understand when institutional or professional help may be necessary.

## Safety-first approach

CalcuLegal Edu modules should be designed with a safety-first logic.

This may include:

* clear disclaimers;
* emergency-resource visibility;
* panic-exit or quick-exit interface patterns;
* age-sensitive language;
* avoidance of blame or escalation;
* evidence-preservation reminders;
* encouragement to contact trusted adults, schools, institutions or professionals where appropriate.

The public documentation describes these principles at a methodological level only.
