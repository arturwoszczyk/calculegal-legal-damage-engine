# Radar Reputazione & Diffamazione

## Live tool

The live orientative tool is available on the official CalcuLegal website:

* [Radar Reputazione & Diffamazione](https://www.calculegal.it/radar-reputazione-diffamazione)
* [Defamation & Reputation Damage Calculator](https://www.calculegal.it/en/defamation-reputation-damage-calculator/)

The live tool is designed for end users who need a preliminary, structured and orientative understanding of reputational harm, defamation risk, visibility, evidence and urgency before deciding whether to seek professional evaluation.

## Purpose

Radar Reputazione & Diffamazione is an additional CalcuLegal tool focused on reputational harm, defamation risk, visibility, evidence, urgency and possible response awareness.

The tool is designed to help users understand:

* whether an online or offline statement may be reputationally harmful;
* whether evidence should be preserved;
* whether urgency exists;
* whether visibility, persistence or virality may increase risk;
* whether sensitive data or identifiable personal information may be involved;
* whether professional evaluation may be appropriate.

It is not a tool for determining whether defamation legally occurred, proving unlawful conduct, ordering content removal, calculating a final compensation amount or replacing legal, forensic, communication or platform-specific evaluation.

## Keywords

defamation, reputation damage, reputational harm, online defamation, offline defamation, digital reputation, content removal, evidence preservation, urgency, visibility, virality, sensitive data, anonymous author, online statements, reputational risk, preliminary legal awareness

## Not a compensation calculator

Unlike damage calculators focused primarily on monetary estimation, Radar Reputazione & Diffamazione may focus more on severity, evidence, visibility, persistence, urgency and response options.

The tool may support preliminary awareness around reputational-risk factors, but it does not determine legal liability or quantify a binding compensation claim.

## Public methodology

The tool follows a structured pattern:

1. identify the reputational-harm or defamation-risk scenario;
2. collect legally relevant non-identifying inputs;
3. organize the case around publication channel, visibility, statement nature, persistence, evidence and urgency;
4. distinguish between reputational impact, evidentiary needs, possible sensitive-data issues and response-awareness dimensions;
5. apply deterministic reasoning or publicly explainable parameters where appropriate;
6. return an orientative risk or urgency profile;
7. remind the user that professional evaluation remains necessary.

## Public methodology categories

The live tool may guide the user through structured categories such as:

* publication channel;
* online or offline context;
* audience or visibility;
* nature of the statement;
* persistence and virality;
* identity or anonymity of author;
* presence of sensitive data;
* identifiability of the affected person or organization;
* evidence availability;
* screenshots, links, timestamps or witnesses;
* urgency of removal or response;
* previous communications;
* platform or hosting context;
* procedural status or previous actions.

This repository does not publish the complete questionnaire flow or the production logic used by the live platform.

## Output categories

The tool may provide preliminary and orientative outputs such as:

* structured summary of the reputational-risk scenario;
* preliminary severity or urgency profile;
* evidence-preservation checklist;
* indication of factors requiring professional review;
* awareness-oriented explanation of possible next steps;
* clarification of the limits of automated orientation.

Outputs are not intended to determine defamation, prove unlawfulness, order removal, quantify a final claim or replace professional evaluation.

## Output nature

Outputs are preliminary, orientative and non-binding.

They are not legal advice, forensic advice, communication advice, platform-removal advice, a professional opinion, a liability assessment or a guarantee of outcome.

## Legal empowerment role

The purpose of this tool is to help users understand whether a reputational-harm or defamation-risk situation may require further professional evaluation, especially where evidence preservation or urgency may be relevant.

It supports awareness, organization of information and preliminary orientation.

It does not replace:

* a lawyer;
* a digital-forensics expert;
* a reputation-management professional;
* a communication consultant;
* a platform or hosting provider;
* a public authority;
* a court;
* a professional assessment of unlawfulness, liability, evidence, removal strategy or damages.

## Methodological note: reputation, evidence and urgency

Reputational-harm scenarios often involve multiple layers: content, context, visibility, persistence, identifiability, evidence, urgency and possible legal qualification.

The public methodology documents the general structure of the reasoning process and the categories of information that may be relevant to preliminary risk awareness.

This repository does not publish complete internal rules, full severity scoring, response-priority thresholds, platform-specific logic or case-specific legal evaluation rules.
